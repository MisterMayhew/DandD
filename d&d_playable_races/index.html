Your Character Is Waiting To Be Born

The birth and development of a character can be more fun than playing them, with one of the most challenging pieces being the choosing of the race with which you wish to become. Here we will assist you in choosing a D&D Playable Race by determining your desires of the game. With free reign of races, here are a few things to ask yourself:

  • What will your Dungeon Master, or DM, allow?
  • What has the rest of your adventuring party chosen?
  • What would it look like if you could be anyone or anything?
  • What makes you happy?

While we wish what makes us happy would be at the top of the list, we must consider other crucial factors. For example, if your DM is running a campaign for only humans, then it is not advisable to make a Tiefling character, as that would go against the campaign. It is also of great importance to check in with your party to determine what Playable Races they have chosen. The shortest campaigns occur when your character triggers other players into killing your character before you can play through your first dungeon. The answers to these questions may reduce your options but keep in mind that nearly every Playable Race can have a Class. Once your Race is chosen, you can proceed to Class and then finally develop your back-story. Nothing is better than watching a Half-Orc Cleric and a Gnome Barbarian running into battle together, hand on pinky finger.

Now Choose

Dragonborn

  • Pronunciation - Dra-gon-born
  • The best part of dragons and humans: Strong and dangerous.

Dwarf

  • Pronunciation - Dw-arf
  • Bearded folk that drink their own body weight and go for the gold.

Elf

  • Pronunciation - El-f
  • A creature of freedom, protecting their values and those close to them.

Gnome

  • Pronunciation - No-mmm
  • Speaking with small animals, they became a shy race with many secrets.

Half-Elf

  • Pronunciation - Haf-el-f
  • Outcast by the elves and hated by humans, they have started to ascend and fight for their place in the world.

Halfling

  • Pronunciation - Haf-ling
  • Similar to humans however growing to about half their size, halflings make excellent thieves.

Half-Orc

  • Pronunciation - Haf-or-c
  • Born between human and orc, the half-orc brings brute force and a blood-lust unparalleled by any puny human.

Human

  • Pronunciation - Hue-man
  • A standard race of character. Need to know more? Look into a mirror.

Tiefling

  • Pronunciation - Tee-fling
  • The outcome of humans making a deal with the devil. These demonic beings are usually not here for tea.
